    I was born and raised in Townsville. During year 11 I got a school-based Apprenticeship as a Heavy Vehicle Mechanic while finishing my remainder years of school.I went on to complete my Apprenticeship and in 2015 I joined the Australian Army as a Vehicle Mechanic which gave me the opportunity to move around the country.

    Growing up I was big into playing sport mostly soccer, I played it for 10 years of my childhood. I stopped playing soccer when I started working full time as a Mechanic. As I got older my interest in Gaming grew (mostly online gaming) and now it is one of my main hobbies.

    Interest in IT

    Growing up technology has advanced and became a lot more of my everyday life, just about everything is done or can be done online. If I could go back knowing technology would be like this, then I would have chosen a career in IT after school. Towards the end of my Army career, I decided that I wanted a change of careers and since I spend a lot of time on computers and the way the world is going, I thought I would try and pursue a career in IT. I have very limited experience in IT but the main reason for wanting to do IT is to be able to fix and rectify tech problems around me.

    Transitioning out of the Army I had no knowledge on Uni whatsoever. I had spoken to a JCU representative at a transition seminar, and they were able to tell me what I needed to be able to study for a Bachelor of IT. I signed up with Open Universities Australia with the interest of studying for my Bachelor of IT and since I have no previous history of studying, they suggested that I try out a single subject to see what I think and how I go with it. I chose this course because it will help me with starting off my studies for the bachelor and I wanted to do this all online which is why I chose RMIT.

    Over the years I got used to being thrown in the deep end when it came to courses. However, this course started at a nice steady pace. Coming into this course I was expecting to learn the basics of how to identify and fix certain tech problems. If I can leave this course with a basic knowledge of IT and a better understanding of it all then I have achieved the goal that I wanted from this course.
